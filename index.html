How it works

The phone is the product

No complex onboarding. No habit to build. No app to remember to open. Just a phone number.

01

every morning

At a time you set. She asks how you're feeling and what's on your day. Talks under 4 minutes. Logs everything she hears.

02

Every ADHD app fails the same way: it requires the user to remember to open it.

— The Frizz Thesis

  • You know you have a 3pm meeting. You cannot feel it approaching. At 2:58 you look up from a hyperfocus session and scramble.
  • You put your glasses somewhere safe. The safe place is immediately forgotten.
  • One appointment at 2pm makes the entire morning psychologically unavailable. The day vanishes around a single future event.
  • You've downloaded 12 productivity apps. Every one felt like hope on day one. Every one is now an icon you scroll past feeling guilty.
  • You want something that comes to you when things are about to go wrong — not something you have to remember to open.

Meet Penny.
She's not a bot.

Penny is a phone number. She picks up in two rings. She doesn't say "Great!" or "Awesome!" or "I'd be happy to help." She says "Got it." and means it.

Her voice is warm, unhurried — like someone who picked up without checking who was calling.
